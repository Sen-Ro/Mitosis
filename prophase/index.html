Prophase
Prophase is the first stage of mitosis, where the cell begins preparing for division. Several important changes occur during this phase:
  1. Chromatin Condenses into Chromosomes
    • The loose, thread-like chromatin in the nucleus coils and condenses into distinct chromosomes.
    • Each chromosome consists of two identical sister chromatids joined at a region called the centromere.
  2. Nuclear Envelope Breaks Down
    • The nuclear membrane starts to disintegrate, allowing chromosomes to move freely in the cytoplasm.
  3. Spindle Fibers Begin to Form
    • In animal cells, centrosomes move toward opposite poles of the cell.
    • The mitotic spindle, made of spindle fibers, begins to form and will later help separate chromosomes.
  4. Nucleolus Disappears
    • The nucleolus, which produces ribosomes, disappears as the cell shifts focus to division.
