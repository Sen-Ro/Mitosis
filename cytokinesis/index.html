Cytokinesis is the process that follows mitosis, where the cytoplasm divides, resulting in two identical daughter cells.
  1. Cytoplasm Splits
    • The cell membrane pinches inward in animal cells, forming a cleavage furrow.
    • In plant cells, a cell plate forms between the two daughter cells.
  2. Formation of Two Identical Daughter Cells
    • Each new cell receives an identical set of chromosomes and organelles.
    • The daughter cells are now ready to enter interphase and resume normal functions.
  3. Different Mechanisms in Animal and Plant Cells
    • Animal cells use actin filaments to contract and split the cell.
    • Plant cells build a new cell wall using vesicles from the Golgi apparatus.
  4. Completion of the Cell Cycle
    • After cytokinesis, the two daughter cells enter interphase to grow and prepare for the next cycle.
    • Each daughter cell is genetically identical to the original parent cell.
