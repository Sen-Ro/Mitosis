Mitosis

Telophase is the final stage of mitosis, where the cell begins to reverse the changes of prophase and prepare for division into two daughter cells.
  1. New Nuclear Envelopes Form
    • A new nuclear membrane forms around each set of chromosomes.
    • This re-establishes a separate nucleus for each daughter cell.
  2. Chromosomes Uncoil into Chromatin
    • Chromosomes begin to decondense back into loose chromatin.
    • This allows normal cellular functions to resume.
  3. Spindle Fibers Disassemble
    • The mitotic spindle, no longer needed, breaks down.
    • Microtubules return to their normal roles in the cell.
  4. Nucleoli Reappear
    • The nucleolus reforms within each nucleus, preparing to produce ribosomes again.
    • Cell division is almost complete, with only cytokinesis left.
