Metaphase
Metaphase is the second stage of mitosis, where the chromosomes align at the center of the cell, ensuring equal distribution to the daughter cells.
  1. Chromosomes Align at the Metaphase Plate
    • Chromosomes are pulled by spindle fibers and align in a straight line at the center of the cell.
    • This central region is called the metaphase plate.
  2. Spindle Fibers Attach to Chromosomes
    • Each chromosome's centromere is attached to spindle fibers from opposite poles.
    • This ensures proper chromosome separation in the next phase.
  3. Cell Prepares for Chromosome Separation
    • Metaphase ensures all chromosomes are properly aligned before moving to anaphase.
    • If misalignment occurs, the cell can halt division to correct errors.
  4. Checkpoint for Accuracy
    • The cell undergoes a checkpoint to confirm that all chromosomes are properly attached to the spindle.
    • If mistakes are detected, the process pauses to prevent errors in division.
